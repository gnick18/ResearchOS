01 - Who we are

The lab notebook you actually own

For academic researchers and the labs they work in who want a serious electronic lab notebook without handing their data and their grant money to a vendor, ResearchOS is a local-first, open-source research workspace that keeps every note, method, experiment, structure, and file in a plain folder the researcher owns. It replaces a stack of expensive single-purpose tools with one free app. Unlike LabArchives and Benchling, which lock your work inside their cloud and bill per seat, ResearchOS leaves your data on your own disk, stays free and open source, and only charges cost-recovery for the optional cloud storage you choose.

Ownership

Your research lives in a folder you own. Leaving is just closing a folder.

One Workspace

One free app spans the notebook, chemistry, sequences, and stats.

Trust

Open source and validated in public, so you can verify the science.

Full detail in docs/branding/POSITIONING.md. These three pillars anchor every page of copy.

02 - Voice and tone

Plain-spoken, trustworthy, never salesy

We talk to smart people, so we state the reason behind every claim instead of asserting it. We are warm without being cute, and we let BeakerBot carry the personality so the copy stays clear.

The hard rules (non-negotiable)

  • No em-dashes. Use commas, parentheses, or a period split.
  • No mid-sentence colons to introduce a clause or list. Recast it.
  • No emojis anywhere in product or marketing copy.
  • State the why. "Free because it is local-first, so our costs are small," not "free."
  • No AI-speak. No "leverage," "seamless," "revolutionize," "unlock," "cutting-edge."

Voice adjectives, plain-spoken, trustworthy, friendly, confident, non-corporate.

Before (AI-speak)

"ResearchOS leverages a cutting-edge local-first architecture to revolutionize your workflow and unlock seamless data ownership."

After (house voice)

"Your research lives in a folder on your own computer. Nothing uploads unless you choose to, so leaving is just closing a folder."

03 - The mascot

The mascot is always BeakerBot

Wherever a character appears, it is BeakerBot, the friendly sky-blue beaker with a pastel-rainbow liquid. He is the calm, playful face of the product. Never corporate, never aggressive.

Do

  • Use the canonical component frontend/src/components/BeakerBot.tsx or an approved master in brand/.
  • Keep the sky-blue stroke #1AA0E6 and the pastel liquid.
  • Use the bubbling marketing variant components/animations/IntroBeaker.tsx for hero moments.

Do not

  • Hand-draw, recolor, or restyle him.
  • Use a different mascot or a generic "robot."
  • Put him in black or a non-brand color.

The signature rainbow liquid is our most ownable visual. Pastel --brand-rainbow for decoration in light mode (ribbons, frames, fills). Vivid --brand-rainbow-vivid for gradient TEXT (the pastel is too light to read as type) and as the whole-surface rainbow in dark mode. Never a generic red or pink rainbow.

04 - Logo and wordmark

The wordmark

The wordmark is ResearchOS set in Geist 800 with tight tracking (about -0.03em) in ink #111827. The lockup pairs the BeakerBot mark with the wordmark.

  • Mark plus wordmark for banners and headers.
  • Wordmark alone where the mark is shown nearby.
  • Mark alone for avatars, favicons, and tight spaces.
  • Keep clear space around the lockup equal to the height of the beaker.
  • Never stretch, recolor, or add effects to the wordmark.

07 - Iconography

One registry, guard-enforced

Every user-facing icon is a registered inline SVG in frontend/src/components/icons/registry.tsx, used through <Icon name="..." />. No icon libraries, no emojis as icons.

  • Default, fill=none, stroke=currentColor, strokeWidth=2, viewBox 0 0 24 24.
  • Browse the catalog at /dev/icons.
  • Adding a new icon needs Grant's sign-off (it is a verified asset).

The icon guard

A pre-commit hook and a vitest ratchet block any new inline <svg> outside the registry. Exempt directories, where decorative or vendor SVG is allowed:

  • components/icons/
  • components/animations/ (e.g. IntroBeaker)
  • components/showcase/
  • vendor/ , app/dev/icons/

09 - Funding acknowledgment

How we credit our support

Confirmed with the OVCR project manager (Laila Robbins). This standard replaces every current reference to a "UW-Madison RISE Initiative / RISE fellowship," which is the wrong name.

Official program name

UW Distinguished Research Fellowship (or "UW Distinguished Research Fellows Program").

Approved acknowledgment wording (verbatim)

"Support for this research was provided by the University of Wisconsin-Madison, Office of the Vice Chancellor for Research with funding from the Wisconsin Alumni Research Foundation."

Compact form (footers, tight spaces)

"Supported by the UW Distinguished Research Fellowship (UW-Madison OVCR, with WARF funding)."
Logos are not approved yet. Laila gave us the acknowledgment text and the official name. Whether we may display UW-Madison, OVCR, or WARF logos on a commercial product site is still pending confirmation from Cynthia Czajkowski (OVCR). Until she confirms, use the text acknowledgment only, no UW or WARF logos.
