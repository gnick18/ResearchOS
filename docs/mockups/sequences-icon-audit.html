Sequences page icon audit

Every icon below is the exact SVG from the code. Mark the ones that do not read.

Grouped by where it appears in the editor. An icon reused in several spots is listed once, with all its homes noted. Amber pills flag likely confusable pairs.

Look for: icons that say nothing on their own, two glyphs that look alike, and a glyph whose shape fights its meaning.

Top action bar

The document-editing row at the very top of the editor (hidden in read-only / embedded). Undo, Redo, the Copy split button, Cut, Paste, Save.

Undo IconUndo
Label: "Undo" / tooltip "Undo (Cmd+Z)"
Means: undo the last edit, stepping the document back one change. The glyph is an arrow that curves down and to the left and doubles back, the classic "go back" return arrow.
Redo IconRedo
Label: "Redo" / tooltip "Redo (Cmd+Shift+Z)"
Means: redo the edit that was just undone, stepping the document forward one change. The glyph is the mirror of Undo, an arrow that curves down and to the right and doubles back.
Copy IconCopy
Label: "Copy" split button / tooltip "Copy (Cmd+C)"
Means: copy the selected bases (or the whole sequence) to the clipboard. The glyph is two overlapping rounded squares, the universal "duplicate to clipboard" pair of sheets.
looks like: ActionGlyphs.copy (inspector), IconDuplicate (primer dialog) — same two-sheets shape
Cut IconCut
Label: "Cut" / tooltip "Cut (Cmd+X)"
Means: cut the selected bases out of the sequence and onto the clipboard. The glyph is a pair of open scissors, two finger-loops with crossing blades, the editing "cut" metaphor.
looks like: IconEnzymes (Show strip "Enzyme sites") — both are scissors; nearly identical paths
Paste IconPasteTool
Label: "Paste" / tooltip "Paste annotated sequence (Cmd+V)"
Means: paste the clipboard contents (an annotated sequence) in at the cursor. The glyph is a clipboard, a board with a small clip tab at the top, the "what you paste from" surface.
looks like: ActionGlyphs.paste (inspector) — a different clipboard glyph for the same idea
Save IconSave
Label: "Save" / "Saving…" / "Saved" / tooltip "Save (Cmd+S)"
Means: write the edited sequence back to the file on disk (cycling through Save, Saving, Saved). The glyph is a floppy disk, a square with a label flap and a write-protect notch, the legacy "save" symbol.
Split-copy caret Chevron (SequenceEditMenu)
Label: none. The caret on the right half of the Copy split button (and on the menu dropdowns).
Means: open the rest of the copy family (bottom strand, amino acids, FASTA, map image) as a dropdown. The glyph is a downward chevron, a "V" pointing down, the standard "more below / open menu" disclosure mark.
looks like: ChevronDownIcon (Import), IconCaret (Show strip), Chevron (Export), CaretIcon (lineage) — five separate down-chevron defs

Right rail (operations)

The thin always-visible icon strip on the right edge. Each icon + its tiny label opens an inspector panel. Grouped Design then Analyze, then Export + More.

Primers RailIcons.primers
Label: "Primers"
Means: open the primer design and check tools. The glyph is a short horizontal line ending in a forward arrowhead, meant to read as a single oligo annealed to a strand and pointing 5-prime to 3-prime.
looks like: IconPrimers (View tabs), IconOrfs (Show strip) — all arrow-on-a-line glyphs
Cloning RailIcons.cloning
Label: "Cloning"
Means: open the assembly and cloning chemistries (Gibson, Golden Gate, Gateway and friends). The glyph is two small circles joined by a short diagonal link, meant to read as two fragments being joined into one construct.
looks like: RailIcons.tree / ActionGlyphs.tree / lineage TreeGlyph — all "circles joined by lines" three-or-two-node shapes
Cut (rail) RailIcons.cut
Label: "Cut" (restriction-enzyme panel)
Means: open the restriction-enzyme digest tools (where enzymes cut). The glyph is a pair of closed scissors, two finger-loops with the blades crossed into an X, the "cut here" metaphor applied to enzyme cut sites.
looks like: IconCut (top bar), IconEnzymes (Show strip) — three scissors glyphs for two different meanings (edit-cut vs enzyme-cut)
Annotate RailIcons.annotate
Label: "Annotate"
Means: add or auto-detect features (annotate the sequence with known parts). The glyph is a bare checkmark, which reads as "done" or "confirm" rather than "annotate", so the drawing fights the meaning.
looks like: ActionGlyphs.check, the toast success check — a plain check that reads as "done", not "annotate"
Align RailIcons.align
Label: "Align"
Means: compare and align two or more sequences. The glyph is three stacked horizontal lines of ragged length, meant to read as aligned rows of bases stacked one over another.
looks like: ActionGlyphs.list, ActionGlyphs.align (identical path), IconWrapped (Show strip) — four near-identical "stacked lines" glyphs
Protein RailIcons.protein
Label: "Protein"
Means: open the protein properties of a coding feature (the translated product). The glyph is two crossing S-curves forming an X, meant to suggest a pair of protein helix ribbons.
looks like: ActionGlyphs.protein (identical path)
Tree RailIcons.tree
Label: "Tree" (taxonomy / lineage)
Means: open the taxonomy / tree-of-life explorer for the sequence's organism. The glyph is three small nodes joined by branches (two on the left feeding one on the right), meant to read as a phylogenetic tree.
looks like: ActionGlyphs.tree (identical), lineage TreeGlyph, list-row organism glyph, RailIcons.cloning — the most-reused node-graph shape
Export RailIcons.export
Label: "Export"
Means: export the sequence out to a file (GenBank, FASTA, image and so on). The glyph is a downward arrow landing on a short baseline, the standard "download / save out" symbol.
looks like: ActionGlyphs.download (identical), NcbiCloudIcon down-arrow, IconExport (Export menu) — three download-style arrows
More RailIcons.more
Label: "More"
Means: reveal additional, less-common operations that do not fit on the rail. The glyph is three dots in a row, the standard "overflow / more" menu symbol.

Plus ActionGlyphs.plus

Means: add or create a new item inside the open inspector panel (a new primer, feature, fragment and so on). The glyph is a plus sign, the universal "add" mark.
looks like: PlusIcon (sidebar "New") — same plus
List ActionGlyphs.list
Label: "list / show all" panel actions.
Means: show all items as a list (the "list / show all" panel action). The glyph is three stacked horizontal lines with the last one shorter, meant to read as rows in a list.
looks like: ActionGlyphs.align + RailIcons.align (Align's exact shape, last line slightly longer) — list and align are indistinguishable
Layer ActionGlyphs.layer
Label: a layer / target panel action.
Means: a layer or target panel action (focus on or toggle a display layer). The glyph is a concentric outer ring around a center dot, drawn as a target / bullseye.
looks like: IconCircular (Show strip topology), IconMap (View tabs ring) — all ring glyphs
Check ActionGlyphs.check
Label: a confirm / apply panel action.
Means: confirm or apply the current panel action. The glyph is a checkmark, the standard "confirm / done" tick.
looks like: RailIcons.annotate (identical), the toast success check
Refresh ActionGlyphs.refresh
Label: a recompute / refresh panel action.
Means: recompute or refresh the panel result. The glyph is two curved arrows chasing each other in a loop, the standard "refresh / reload" symbol.
Align ActionGlyphs.align
Label: an align panel action.
Means: an align panel action (line sequences up for comparison). The glyph is three stacked horizontal lines of ragged length, meant to read as aligned rows of bases.
looks like: RailIcons.align (byte-identical path), ActionGlyphs.list — three "stacked lines" all here
Protein ActionGlyphs.protein
Label: a protein panel action.
Means: a protein panel action (work with the translated product). The glyph is two crossing S-curves forming an X, meant to suggest protein helix ribbons.
looks like: RailIcons.protein (byte-identical path)
Tree ActionGlyphs.tree
Label: a taxonomy panel action.
Means: a taxonomy panel action (explore the organism's lineage). The glyph is three small nodes joined by branches, meant to read as a phylogenetic tree.
looks like: RailIcons.tree (byte-identical), lineage TreeGlyph, list-row organism glyph
Search ActionGlyphs.search
Label: a find / search panel action.
Means: a find / search panel action (look something up). The glyph is a magnifying glass, the universal "search" symbol.
looks like: the "Find" button glyph (tabs spine) — same magnifier, two copies
Download ActionGlyphs.download
Label: an export / download panel action.
Means: an export / download panel action (save the result out to a file). The glyph is a downward arrow landing on a short baseline, the standard "download" symbol.
looks like: RailIcons.export (byte-identical)
Copy ActionGlyphs.copy
Label: a copy panel action.
Means: a copy panel action (copy the item or value to the clipboard). The glyph is two overlapping rounded squares, the standard "duplicate to clipboard" pair of sheets.
looks like: IconCopy (top bar), IconDuplicate (primer dialog) — all two-sheet glyphs
Paste ActionGlyphs.paste
Label: a paste panel action.
Means: a paste panel action (drop the clipboard contents into the panel). The glyph is a clipboard with a down arrow inside it, meant to read as "paste into here".
looks like: IconPasteTool (top bar) — a different clipboard for the same idea
Pencil ActionGlyphs.pencil
Label: an edit / rename panel action.
Means: edit or rename the item (an edit panel action). The glyph is a pencil drawn at an angle, the universal "edit" symbol.
Inspector close SequenceOperationsRail X
Label: aria "Collapse the inspector" / tooltip "Collapse the inspector"
Means: collapse the open inspector panel back down to the thin rail. The glyph is an X (two crossing lines), the standard "close / dismiss" mark.
looks like: the toast dismiss X (two crossing lines) — same X, different markup
Context-bar filled marker InspectorContextBar
Label: sits before "Acting on selection..." text (amber bar).
Means: the upcoming operation will act only on the current selection (the amber "Acting on selection" state). The glyph is a solid filled disc, a filled-in dot signalling an active, narrowed target.
Context-bar hollow marker InspectorContextBar
Label: sits before "Whole sequence" text (calm bar).
Means: the upcoming operation acts on the whole sequence because nothing is selected (the calm "Whole sequence" state). The glyph is a hollow outlined square, an empty box signalling a wide, unfilled target.

View tabs (bottom)

The SnapGene-style bottom tab bar that switches the main view. Map, Sequence, Features, Primers, History (each icon + a text label).

Map IconMap
Label: "Map" / tooltip "Whole-molecule map view"
Means: switch to the whole-molecule map view (the SnapGene-style plasmid overview). The glyph is a circle with four short ticks pointing inward at the compass points, meant to read as a circular plasmid map.
looks like: the "Map view" badge glyph + the circular-zoom indicator (identical ring+ticks path)
Sequence IconSequence
Label: "Sequence" / tooltip "Base-level sequence view"
Means: switch to the base-level view that shows the actual letters of the sequence. The glyph is a few stylized letter shapes sitting on a baseline, standing in for the A C G T text.
Features IconFeatures
Label: "Features" + count / tooltip "Feature list and editing"
Means: switch to the feature list and editing view (with a count of features). The glyph is a price tag, a rectangle tapering to a point with a small hole, the common "label / tag" metaphor for an annotated feature.
Primers IconPrimers
Label: "Primers" + count / tooltip "Primer list and design"
Means: switch to the primer list and design view (with a count of primers). The glyph is a short forward arrow sitting above a baseline, meant to read as an oligo annealed to a strand.
looks like: RailIcons.primers (the rail's Primers icon) — both arrow-on-line, two different drawings
History IconHistory
Label: "History" / tooltip "Edit and version history"
Means: switch to the edit and version-history view (browse and restore past states). The glyph is a clock face with a counter-clockwise arrow sweeping around it, the standard "rewind through time / history" symbol.

Show strip

The horizontal "Show" chip row above the viewer. Each chip toggles a display layer. Plus the feature-types flyout caret and its eye toggles.

Enzyme sites IconEnzymes
Label: "Enzyme sites" / tooltip "Show or hide restriction-enzyme cut sites"
Means: toggle the restriction-enzyme cut sites on or off in the viewer. The glyph is a pair of open scissors, the "where enzymes cut" metaphor.
looks like: IconCut (top bar) — byte-identical scissors path for a different action
Translation IconTranslation
Label: "Translation" / tooltip "Show or hide the amino-acid translation of CDS features"
Means: toggle the amino-acid translation of CDS features on or off under the bases. The glyph is a box holding two "A" letter shapes, standing in for the amino-acid (protein) text.
Open reading frames IconOrfs
Label: "Open reading frames" / tooltip about ATG-to-stop runs
Means: toggle the open reading frames on or off (the ATG-to-stop runs on each strand). The glyph is two opposed arrows, one pointing right and one pointing left, meant to show reading frames running in both directions.
looks like: RailIcons.primers, IconPrimers — all directional-arrow glyphs
Ruler / index IconRuler
Label: "Ruler / index" / tooltip "Show or hide the ruler / index row"
Means: toggle the coordinate ruler / index row on or off (the base-position numbers). The glyph is a horizontal ruler bar with evenly spaced tick marks, the literal measuring-ruler metaphor.
Circular IconCircular
Label: "Circular" / tooltip "Show as circular" (topology chip)
Means: set the molecule topology to circular (treat the sequence as a closed plasmid). The glyph is a plain unbroken ring, standing for the circular DNA loop.
looks like: ActionGlyphs.layer (ring), IconMap (ring+ticks) — bare ring reads as "map" too
Linear IconLinear
Label: "Linear" / tooltip "Show as linear" (topology chip)
Means: set the molecule topology to linear (treat the sequence as an open fragment with two ends). The glyph is a horizontal line with a short vertical cap at each end, standing for a linear strand with free ends.
looks like: the list-row MoleculeIcon linear branch (line) — close in spirit
Wrapped IconWrapped
Label: "Wrapped" / tooltip "Switch to a single continuous line"
Means: wrap the sequence into stacked rows so it reads top-to-bottom like paragraphs. The glyph is three stacked horizontal lines with the last one shorter, meant to read as wrapped rows of bases.
looks like: RailIcons.align, ActionGlyphs.list / align — same three-lines family. Confusable with Align especially.
Single line IconSingleLine
Label: "Single line" / tooltip "Switch to wrapped rows"
Means: show the sequence on one continuous horizontal row that scrolls left and right (the opposite of Wrapped). The glyph is a single row with a double-headed horizontal arrow, meant to read as "one long line you scroll sideways".
Feature-types caret IconCaret
Label: none. The disclosure caret on the right half of the Features chip.
Means: open the flyout that shows or hides each individual feature type. The glyph is a downward chevron drawn with a heavier stroke, the standard "open this dropdown" disclosure mark.
looks like: Chevron (Copy/Export), ChevronDownIcon (Import), CaretIcon (lineage) — yet another down chevron
Eye (shown) IconEye
Label: per-type row in the Feature-types flyout (also reused in the Edit menu toggle rows).
Means: this feature type is currently shown (click to toggle it off). The glyph is an open eye with a pupil, the standard "visible" symbol.
looks like: the SequenceEditMenu IconEye (byte-identical) — two defs
Eye off (hidden) IconEyeOff
Label: per-type row in the Feature-types flyout (also reused in the Edit menu toggle rows).
Means: this feature type is currently hidden (click to toggle it back on). The glyph is an eye with a diagonal slash through it, the standard "hidden / not visible" symbol.
looks like: the SequenceEditMenu IconEyeOff (byte-identical) — two defs

Sidebar and list
