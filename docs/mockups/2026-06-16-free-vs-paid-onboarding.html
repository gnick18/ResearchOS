Onboarding funnel, Free vs Paid (Model A)

The fix for the no-solo-paid-path problem. A solo researcher now sees a real paid option that is not "become a lab."

The problem (current chooser)

Free account

Receive shares, directory.

Lab Paid

Create or join a lab.

A solo researcher's only paid door is "create a lab," which they will never click. The paid Solo tier is invisible, so a solo user lands on Free and never converts.

The new front door

Free Stay in the network

$0, no card. Make an account or keep working locally.
  • Unlimited local notebook, your data on your disk
  • Receive notes, methods, and files others share with you
  • Be findable in the researcher directory
  • Accept folder and lab invites
  • One-time gift of about 1.6M AI tokens on sign-up
  • Send or co-edit your own work (paid)
  • Phone capture sync (paid)

Paid Produce and collaborate

Pay for what you use, a small base plus your actual cloud usage. A cap you set means no surprises.
Solo$3/mo + usage

Just you. Send and co-edit your work, phone capture, push. Learn more

Lab$25/mo per lab + usage

Your team together. Shared workspace, real-time co-editing, PI billing. Learn more

Department$50/mo per lab + usage

Many labs. Governance, compliance, one consolidated invoice. Learn more

Sequencing keeps your locked "free wizard first" intent: a brand-new visitor can still hit "Start free" and explore with no login. This Free-vs-Paid screen is the choice moment, and "Start free" still leads to the account pitch (1.6M token gift + AI-tailored onboarding) or staying local.

The gentle nudge (for users who chose Free)
My experiment, lab notes
↗
Sending shares this beyond your folder

That is a Solo feature, $3/mo plus the small bit of cloud you use, with a cap you set. Your local copy never changes.

Restraint rules: only on a real produce action the user themselves initiated (clicking Send or Co-edit), never a banner or interrupt. At most once every few weeks, dismissible, and "Not now" is remembered. It explains the feature they reached for, so it reads as helpful, not a sales pitch.

Every dollar figure here comes from the canonical catalog (lib/billing/catalog) in the real build, so a price change updates this screen too. Numbers are the Model A working config.
