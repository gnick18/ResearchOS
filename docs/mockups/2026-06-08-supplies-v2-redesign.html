2. Inventory → interaction parity#parity

Purchases already has a full BeakerSearch source + right-click PI menu. Inventory has none. To let either page shed permanent UI, Inventory needs the same. Proposal: an Inventory beaker source + a right-click menu on item/stock rows + hover-as-context + a deep-link. Keep Inventory's strong inline controls (tap status, count steppers) AND add the off-page paths.

3. The fork — how unified?#route

Inventory and Purchases share identity fields (name / vendor / catalog # / CAS / url) but have orthogonal state: on-hand (count, status, expiry, location) vs ordering (order-status, price, funding, approval). There's even an unused FK linking a received purchase to its stock. Two ways to exploit that.

Route B preview is in section 4. Pick here; mark section 4 on whether the unified design clears the bar.
4. Route B preview — one Supplies list#unified

One list. Identity is entered once. On-hand state (from Inventory) and ordering state (from Purchases) ride as two badge clusters per row. Filters answer the two daily questions: "what needs attention" and "what needs ordering". The spending plots are a lab-head drawer; approval/funding move into the detail panel + lab-head views.

A "purchase-only" thing (flights, services) has just the Ordering section. A "never-ordered" stock has just On hand. Most reagents have both. Receiving an order auto-creates / increments its on-hand stock (the FK that exists today).
Honest tradeoffs: Purchases is task-scoped with funding + multi-item orders + PI approval; folding that into a per-supply row needs care (an "order" groups items; approval is per-item). Route B is a real data-model reconciliation, not just a view swap. Route A gets 70% of the benefit at 30% of the risk.
