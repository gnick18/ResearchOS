Slimming the global navigation bar

Design review. The current bar versus four ways to reclaim the vertical space it eats on every screen.

This is a design mockup, not the real app. Every signed-in page carries a persistent global header with eleven top-level tabs, the wordmark and streak badge, and right-side controls. It eats a fixed strip of vertical space everywhere. Each option below sits on the same sample page (a Methods explorer) so the bar-versus-content ratio is an honest comparison. Pick a direction here and we build it.

The current bar Before

Full-width header, eleven text tabs, the wordmark and a streak badge on the left, four right-side icon controls (timer, phone pairing, settings, your avatar). This is the baseline. The tab strip is rendered identically in every option so the savings are visible.

Option 1, slim in place Option 1 Simplest, lowest risk

The same horizontal bar, just shorter. Tighter vertical padding, a smaller wordmark, tighter tab spacing and font, smaller right-side icons. Nothing moves, nothing hides, so there is no relearning. The cheapest win.

Reclaims about 10px of height

Option 2, auto-hide on scroll Option 2

The full bar shows at the top. Scroll the sample content down and the bar slides up out of view, scroll up and it comes back, the common app pattern. A thin accent line stays pinned at the very top while the bar is hidden so you always know it is one scroll-up away. Scroll inside the framed area below to feel it.

Reclaims the full bar (about 53px) while reading, returns on intent

Option 3, collapse to a left rail Option 3 Frees the most space

The tabs leave the top entirely and live in a thin 56px icon rail on the left edge. Hover the rail and it expands to show labels. The top strip then carries only the wordmark and the right-side controls, so the whole tab row of vertical space is gone. Hover the rail below to expand it.

Reclaims the entire tab row from the top (about 36px), tabs move to the side
