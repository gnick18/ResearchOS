section 6 · spotlight
// your lab, in your pocket
The companion app brings ResearchOS to the bench
Most of research happens standing at a bench, not sitting at a desk. The companion app on your phone is the bench end of your notebook, so the messy steps flow straight into the experiment on your computer. Nothing to retype later.
companion-app.mp4 (new)
Snap a photo into the experiment
Photograph a gel, a plate, or the bench and it lands in the right experiment on your computer at full resolution. No cable, no retyping.
Scan handwritten notes to text
Point your phone at a page of bench scrawl and it pulls the text out, so a paper note becomes a searchable entry in the experiment.
Scan a barcode, inventory updates itself
Scan the barcode on a reagent box and inventory deducts automatically as you use it. No spreadsheet, no manual count, the stock stays right.
Run methods on your phone, not on paper
Open a method in reading mode and follow it step by step at the bench instead of printing it. Add a variation note from your phone and it saves back to the run.

section 9
// why we built this
Research software should be accessible and better, not expensive and locked

ResearchOS is an open-source company (AGPLv3) that grew out of a research fellowship at UW-Madison. The tools labs depend on are overpriced, and they hold your data hostage in someone else's cloud. We build free, open, local-first alternatives, and the goal is not just cheaper, it is better: you own your data, the science is validated in public, and a real, accountable business stands behind it. Good research tooling should be a public good, inspectable, ownable, and free.

Dr. Grant Nickles, founder · Built in Madison, Wisconsin
