Outbox redesign — manual outbox → automatic sync

What is the outbox FOR?

When you snap a bench photo or jot a quick note, the app sends it to your lab the moment you confirm, then asks where to file it. It lands on your laptop within seconds. The list of recent captures is there to reassure you that it arrived, and to give you a way out if something fails.

But the current UI describes that automatic process with manual-process words. "Outbox" implies a staging area you flush yourself. "Queued" implies it is waiting on you. A blue "Send" button on every row implies you have to tap it, when sending already happened, that button is really a retry for the rare stuck item. The first-time read is "wait, do I need to press Send?"

Proposed purpose. Call it the Inbox (the same word the laptop uses for where these land) and make it honest about being automatic. Each row just shows where it is (Sending, On your laptop, Waiting for connection); a per-row action appears only on a real failure (Retry). The header keeps small, low-key bulk conveniences, Send all (push any stragglers now) and Remove all (clear the inbox), instead of a big full-width button. A colorful inbox badge gives the section energy instead of reading grey and sad.

Section named Inbox to match the laptop (the destination), with a colorful sky badge so it is not grey and sad. The action button says "Send to Inbox" so the direction stays clear. Small inline Send all (sky) and Remove all (coral) replace the big button. All captures sit in one container with hairline separators (the ideal look), lighter titles, each row just shows where it is. Retry appears only on a real failure, and a swipe deletes a single row.
