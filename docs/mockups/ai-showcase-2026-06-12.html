BeakerBot AI

Your research data.
An agent that does real work.

BeakerBot is a browser-side AI agent built into ResearchOS. When you ask it a question, it picks the right tool, runs the computation locally against your own data, and hands you the result, not just a chat message.

750,000 tokens free, no card needed.
That covers about 20 to 25 full analyses or 100-plus quick questions. After that, top-ups are priced at cost ($10 / $25 / $50). You see your balance and exactly what each task cost.
Data Hub: statistics and publication figures
A GraphPad Prism alternative, built local-first. BeakerBot picks the right test for your design, runs it in the browser against your own table, and gives you the numbers, an assumption report card, and the equivalent Python so you can reproduce the result anywhere.
G
Run a t-test on the growth data in my OD600 table. WT versus knockout, six replicates each.
run_datahub_analysis ~30,000 tokens

Sequences: primers, Tm, translation, and more
A SnapGene-style sequence workbench, but the AI does the legwork. Ask for primers in plain language and get back designed oligos with nearest-neighbor Tm values, GC content, and a self-complementarity check, without leaving your notes.
G

Search your own work: cross-type, context-aware
BeakerBot searches across every type of object in your folder (notes, experiments, sequences, methods, and molecules) and pulls back actual snippets from the relevant records. It is the same engine that powers BeakerSearch, but driven by a natural-language question instead of a keyword.

No subscription. No seat fees. You see every token.

AI on ResearchOS is metered at cost, we pass through the compute at a thin buffer, we do not mark it up like a subscription product. The reason is that a lab's AI usage varies a lot month to month. One week of heavy analysis should not cost the same as four quiet weeks.

After the free gift runs out, top-ups are $10, $25, or $50. Your balance and per-task cost are always visible in the sidebar.
