Pricing
The notebook is free. You pay only for optional cloud storage.

ResearchOS runs on your own computer, so your everyday research never touches our servers. The local notebook is free and open source forever. Cloud storage is the one optional add on, priced to cover what it costs us. Larger institutions pay a little above that, and the surplus keeps ResearchOS free for individual researchers.

Billing is off during the beta, so every plan is free right now.
Two parts, and only one of them ever costs money

Knowing which part is which is the whole pricing model. The reason cloud stays cheap is the local-first design, your daily work never leaves your disk.

Your local notebook

Free and open source forever

Your notes, methods, experiments and files live in a folder on your own disk, under the AGPLv3 license. No account, no internet, nothing to pay. This is the whole app, not a limited trial.

Optional cloud storage

Low cost, only what it costs us

Only the documents you choose to share or co-edit in real time get a synced cloud copy. That copy is the one thing we charge for, and only to cover the storage. Local-first keeps those costs small, so the price stays small too.

Why the review is easy. We are local-first and end-to-end encrypted, so we hold almost none of your institution's data, and what we relay we cannot read. The code is open source, so your IT office can audit it, and sign-in already works through Microsoft Entra. A tool that stores everyone's research in one place has a far longer review than one that stores almost nothing.

How we would price it, our actual costs

No guessing. Here is the real math, our infrastructure cost plus payment processing plus a small buffer. Individuals and labs pay exactly this, and larger institutions pay a transparent bit more that funds the free tiers. Everything is free during the beta.

How we keep the cost tiny

Cold storage, Cloudflare R2$0.015 / GB / mo
Active sync layer, hot$0.35 / GB / mo

Almost all of your synced data rests in cheap cold storage, with only a thin active layer on the faster, pricier tier. That blend puts our all-in cost near $0.05 per GB per month, far below either tier on its own. Local-first is why, your everyday work never leaves your disk, so very little ever needs the hot layer.

What this means for you

Most individual researchers never pay. The free 5 GB pool covers typical note and image sharing, so you stay on Free. Share a lot of large datasets and it is a few dollars a month, never charged per keystroke.

Labs pool that free tier across the whole team. Departments and institutions use the builders above, where the same blended cost scales up, plus a small sustaining contribution that keeps all of this free for the individual researchers.

An estimate, not a final price, and free today during the beta. Tax is added on top only where the LLC is registered to collect it, which for most universities is nowhere.

Your editing is never metered

We never charge per keystroke or per sync. Other tools nickel and dime every action, and we built the opposite on purpose.

Editing is included, not billed

Collaboration and editing come with your plan. There is no second meter watching what you type, and no per-edit charge ever appears on your invoice.

A throttle, never a surprise bill

If a very heavy month runs past your editing allowance, real-time sync slows to periodic sync. Your work keeps saving. If a lab keeps hitting it, the PI raises the plan, nobody gets a shock charge.

Your editing is never metered.

Built for a whole lab, billed to one person

A lab plan is a shared pool for the team, not a charge per head. The PI runs it and members just use it.

One shared pool, one invoice

The free tier and any paid plan are a single pool for the whole lab. Only the PI pays, on one consolidated invoice. Members are never billed and never enter a card.

Invite by email, member accepts

The PI invites a member by email, and the member accepts before the lab starts covering them. We do not store the email address permanently.

The PI can manage the pool

Because the PI pays, they can see each member's storage and activity use, so they can manage the shared pool. Members are told this when they accept.

Local-first for everyone

Every member still keeps their own data in their own folder. The lab plan funds the shared, synced copies, not the local work, which stays free.

We cannot run up a bill and hand it to you

Two guardrails make that a promise we can keep, not a slogan.

A cost circuit breaker

We set a hard monthly budget. If cloud spend ever approaches it, cloud writes pause and the local-first app keeps working with zero interruption. There is no runaway bill that we then pass to you.

Priced to sustain, not to profit

Individuals and labs pay what storage costs us, no more. Larger institutions pay a modest sustaining rate above cost, and that surplus keeps ResearchOS free for individual researchers and funds the open-source development. We are not extracting profit, we are keeping a public good alive.
