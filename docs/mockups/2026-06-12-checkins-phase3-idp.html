The Individual Development Plan Core differentiator

Five sections, modeled on AAAS myIDP plus the UW-Madison Grad School IDP (one shared lineage), deliberately lighter than the full 15-page UW packet. It is the trainee's document. The mentor reviews and signs off, the trainee edits. NIH progress reports now expect an IDP to exist, so this is a compliance hook, not just a nicety.

M Mira's development planYou mentor Mira here. She owns this plan; you review it.
One form, filtered by stage. Switching hides or surfaces rows, it does not load a different form.
Compliance hook. Your lab-head view shows only "IDP on file, last updated Jun 4," never the contents. NSF has required since May 2024 that an IDP exists. The why, the plan stays the trainee's so candid self-assessment stays safe.

Privacy and what the PI sees Core

The IDP is private by default, local-first like a note. The trainee shares specific sections (usually Goals and the Action plan) and can withhold raw self-ratings or a non-academic career interest. The mentor gets a review surface, the lab head gets a status line only.

Action plan (shared by Mira)
Raw self-ratings (kept private by Mira)

IDP on file, last updated Jun 4, 2026
Self-assessment ratings hidden from this view
Career aspirations hidden from this view
Goal detail hidden from this view
The PI never sees the contents, only that a plan exists and when it was touched (NSF compliance).

Career-stage-aware check-in templates Core

About six seed templates from static JSON, the way the method catalog works, not a 500-template marketplace. Each seeds the agenda and IDP defaults for a stage or a relationship type.

Six seeds, not a marketplace. New ones ship as static JSON, the same pattern as the method catalog.

Expectations compact and onboarding checklist Core

Misaligned expectations is the number one cause of mentoring breakdowns (CIMER). A one-time compact per relationship and a first-check-in onboarding checklist head that off.
