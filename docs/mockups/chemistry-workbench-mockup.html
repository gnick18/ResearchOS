Chemistry / Import from PubChem

Search PubChem

The same browser-direct pattern as the NCBI sequence import. Search a public government database, preview the metadata, import the structure into your library in one click.

The only thing that leaves your browser is the name you type, sent to PubChem, a public NIH resource. No account, no proxy, no tracking. You receive back a public structure.

Chemistry / Find in literature

Find in literature LIVE DATA

The free answer to the SciFinder feature chemists pay for. Pick a compound or draw a fragment, see the papers and patents that mention it. Every result below is fetched live, right now, from PubChem, Europe PMC, and SureChEMBL, all browser-direct with no backend.

Search patents by substructure SureChEMBL, 28M patents

This is the capability PubChem does not have. Enter a SMILES or SMARTS fragment, SureChEMBL finds compounds extracted from patent text and images that contain it. Async, it submits a search and polls, the same as a real cheminformatics server.

Honest limit, stated up front. PubChem links are curated depositor + co-occurrence data. Europe PMC mines open-access and CC-licensed full text plus all abstracts, not every paywalled paper. SureChEMBL indexes specific compounds extracted from patents, not the broad generic Markush claims that CAS deconstructs. We show what is free and say what it is not. We do not replace CAS curation, we make the free 90 percent one click away.
