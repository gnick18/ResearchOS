Open design questions — Section 1

Q1. ORCID sync depthShould the publication list auto-refresh from the public ORCID API (no user action needed, ~80% coverage) or require the user to OAuth-connect their ORCID account (100% coverage, higher friction)?
Q2. Bio length280 characters (quick scan, mocked here) vs 500 characters (space for a real intro). Both feel reasonable; 280 disciplines researchers to be terse.
Q3. Connection label"Colleague" vs "Collaborator" — which fits the ResearchOS voice? "Colleague" is mocked here; it implies symmetric social parity. "Collaborator" implies a research relationship but may not be accurate for mentors/mentees.

Open design questions — Section 5

Q5. Change sign-in grace periodWhen switching providers (e.g. grad student moving from .edu to personal Gmail), should both providers work in parallel for a window (e.g. 30 days) or should it be an atomic swap?
Q6. Invite link expiryHow long should the one-time invite link be valid for someone who has not yet joined ResearchOS? 7 days? 30 days? Should the sender be able to set this?
Q7. Lab mates tier sourceLab mates are inferred from shared lab membership in ResearchOS. Should this tier only appear when the sender and recipient share an active lab folder, or also for alumni of a shared lab?
