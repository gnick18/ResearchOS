Relapse-free survival, FakeDrug-A vs vehicle · Cox proportional hazards
The hazard of relapse for the Vehicle control arm is 6.24 times that of FakeDrug-A (95% CI 2.57 to 15.18, p < 0.0001). The model concordance is 0.72.
BeakerBot
FakeDrug-A roughly halves the hazard of relapse. A hazard ratio compares how fast the event happens between arms. Here the Vehicle arm relapses 6.24x faster than FakeDrug-A (95% CI 2.57 to 15.18, p < 0.0001), and the CI stays well above 1, so the protection is statistically solid. Concordance 0.72 means the model ranks who relapses first correctly about 72% of the time.
For your numbers: HR 6.24 means that at any moment, an untreated subject is about 6x as likely to relapse next as a treated one. Flip the reference arm to read it as FakeDrug-A's protective HR of ~0.16.
