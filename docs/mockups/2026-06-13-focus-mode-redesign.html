Focus mode redesign — “Writing Room”

LiveMarkdownEditor focus mode · before / after · type in the After panel to feel it

Today’s focus mode is the normal editor dropped onto a flat surface with a permanent utilitarian toolbar — a 4/10. This redesign turns it into a writing room: a calm ambient canvas, chrome that dissolves while you write, typewriter scrolling, and optional focus dimming. Click into the After panel and start typing — the toolbar dozes off, the active paragraph stays lit, and the line you’re on holds its place on screen. The centered column leaves big dead gutters on a laptop, so the left gutter is an Insert rail (drop embeds at your caret) and the right gutter is a Context rail (outline, placed embeds, attachments, BeakerBot) — both doze with the chrome. Every change below is independently approvable.
