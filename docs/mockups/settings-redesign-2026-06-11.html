Settings redesign (unified)

One left-rail destination. Click a rail item to swap the pane. Comment mode pins notes, approve each change below, Export.

Today settings is fragmented. A 3,774-line /settings page with Personal and Lab tabs, a separate /profile page, a standalone public-profile card, and three avatar-menu entries that each go somewhere different. This redesign collapses all of it into one left-rail destination, the modern Linear / Vercel / Notion shape, with a grouped sidebar on the left and a focused content pane on the right. The avatar menu becomes a single "Settings" link. The flagship new section is Usage and billing, the first honest, plain-language readout of AI tokens and cloud storage in one place.

Before, fragmented

  • /settings, 3,774 lines, blocky cards stacked under Personal and Lab tabs, everything scrolls in one wall
  • /profile, a separate page for appearance, researcher profile, and ORCID
  • A standalone public-profile card living apart from both
  • Three avatar-menu entries pointing at different destinations, so "where do I change this" has no single answer
  • No single place to see AI usage or storage cost

After, one destination

  • Left rail plus a content pane, sections grouped under YOU, USAGE AND BILLING, WORKSPACE, DATA, and LAB
  • /profile folds fully in as Profile and appearance, the public-profile card included
  • The avatar menu collapses to one Settings link
  • Grouped rows with quiet dividers, label left and control right, not boxy stacked cards
  • A new Usage and billing dashboard, AI tokens in plain language plus cloud storage and plan
