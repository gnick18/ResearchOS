Chrome v5 — the top bar is just the document, the rail is every tool

The overlap you spotted: the top Primer / Enzyme / Feature / Export dropdowns do the same family of things as the right rail, so nobody can tell them apart. Fix: remove them from the top. The top bar becomes pure document editing; the rail (which you like) becomes the single home for every tool. One rule, no guessing.

  • Document (top): what you do to the letters. Undo/Redo, Copy/Cut/Paste, Save. Always the same, never a tool.
  • Tools (right rail): what you do to the sequence as a molecule. Every bioinformatics family lives here, and the object-level edits (edit this primer, delete this feature, choose enzymes) surface inside the rail panel for that family and on right-click.
  • One sentence to remember the whole editor: the top edits the bases, the right does the science.
