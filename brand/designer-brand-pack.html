ResearchOS . Brand pack for designers

Meet BeakerBot.

This is the toolkit for designing ResearchOS merch. The goal is simple: take BeakerBot as your muse and reinterpret him in any art style you love. Everything you need (the mascot, the colors, the fonts, the logo) is below, and the editable vector files are in the assets/ folder next to this page.

Who he is

BeakerBot is a friendly lab beaker with a face: two round eyes, a little smile, a tiny antenna, and a beaker full of pastel rainbow liquid. He is curious, warm, and never corporate. He is the personality of the whole brand, so the copy can stay plain and the science can stay serious.

01 . The creative brief

Reimagine him, keep him recognizable

Use BeakerBot as inspiration and recreate him in whatever style fits the piece: sticker art, embroidery, retro print, line art, pixel, risograph, sketch, 3D, whatever is cool. The one job is that someone who knows ResearchOS still goes "that's BeakerBot." Keep his three signatures and you have full freedom on everything else.

Keep these three
1. The beaker silhouette. 2. A friendly face (eyes + smile). 3. The pastel rainbow liquid inside.
Go wild on
Style, texture, linework, extra props (goggles, lab coat, test tubes), scenes, color treatment of the glass and outline.

02 . The mascot rules

Do and do not

Keep the liquid a soft pastel rainbow (peach, lemon, mint, sky, lilac), it is always pastel, even on dark merch.
Do not use the saturated logo rainbow for his liquid, and do not make him a different vessel (flask, tube, mug). He is a beaker.
Keep him friendly and approachable, a smile reads best.
Do not make him scary, angry, or photoreal-creepy, and do not swap in a different mascot. The mascot is always BeakerBot.
Recolor the glass/outline freely for a style (gold-line, black-line, neon, etc.).
Do not stretch or skew him so the beaker proportions break.

04 . Type

Geist

The brand font is Geist (free, open source, on Google Fonts). The wordmark is Geist Extrabold (800) with tight tracking. Body is Geist Regular/Medium. Code or data uses Geist Mono.

05 . The logo

Wordmark

One wordmark, "ResearchOS", set in Geist Extrabold. "Research" takes the surface text color; "OS" always carries the rainbow (vivid on light, luminous on dark). The mark (BeakerBot) may sit to the left of the wordmark as a lockup, or stand alone.

06 . What's in the folder

Asset map

assets/mascot/ — BeakerBot as vector SVG: the core mark, a solo version with shadow, "at a computer" and "in the lab" scenes, the favicon mark, and four colorway discs (peach, sky, white, purple).

assets/logo/ — the wordmark and the mark+wordmark lockup, each in a light and a dark version (transparent), plus PNGs rendered in real Geist.

assets/color/ — the pastel, vivid, and luminous rainbow ramps as SVG swatches for reference.

SVGs are plain vector, open them in Illustrator, Figma, Affinity, or Inkscape. The mascot is built from simple paths, so it is easy to restyle.
